The PLAY Project

The PLAY Project (Play and Language for Autistic Youngsters) is an evidence-based early intervention program designed to help children with autism spectrum disorders develop social communication skills through play.

What is The PLAY Project?

The PLAY Project uses a relationship-based approach that focuses on following the child's lead, building on their natural interests and motivations. Through play-based activities, children learn to engage socially, communicate effectively, and develop meaningful relationships.

This innovative program recognizes that children with autism learn best when they are engaged, motivated, and having fun. By meeting children where they are and building on their strengths and interests, The PLAY Project helps children develop the foundational skills needed for social communication and learning.

How The PLAY Project Works

1

Individual assessment to understand your child's unique strengths, challenges, and developmental profile using standardized tools and clinical observation.

2

Customized Intervention Plan

Development of an individualized intervention plan based on your child's specific needs, interests, and your family's priorities and goals.

3

Parent Coaching and Training

Comprehensive parent education and coaching to help you implement PLAY Project strategies during everyday activities and routines at home.

4

Ongoing Support and Monitoring

Regular progress monitoring, plan adjustments, and continued coaching to ensure your child is making meaningful progress toward their goals.

Benefits of The PLAY Project

Enhanced Social Communication

Improved ability to engage with others, share attention, and communicate needs and wants effectively.

Increased Engagement

Greater participation in family activities, improved attention span, and increased motivation to interact with others.

Emotional Regulation

Better self-regulation skills, reduced challenging behaviors, and improved ability to cope with changes and transitions.

Family Empowerment

Parents gain confidence and skills to support their child's development throughout daily routines and activities.

Improved Quality of Life

Enhanced family relationships, reduced stress, and increased enjoyment of everyday activities together.

Long-term Development

Building foundational skills that support continued learning and development throughout your child's life.
