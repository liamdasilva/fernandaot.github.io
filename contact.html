Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my child needs occupational therapy?

If your child is struggling with daily activities like dressing, eating, playing, or learning, occupational therapy might help. Common signs include difficulty with fine motor skills, sensory sensitivities, attention challenges, or delayed development.

Do I need a referral from a doctor?

No referral is required for private occupational therapy services. However, some insurance plans may require a doctor's referral for coverage. I can help you understand your specific requirements.

What age children do you work with?

I work with infants, children, and adolescents from birth through 18 years of age. My approach is tailored to each child's developmental stage and individual needs.

Where do sessions take place?

Sessions can take place in your home, community settings, or schools, depending on your child's needs and goals. I believe in providing therapy in natural environments where skills will be used.

How long is the typical treatment process?

Treatment length varies based on individual needs and goals. Some children benefit from short-term intervention, while others may need longer-term support. We'll discuss this during our initial consultation.

Do you accept insurance?

I accept extended health insurance and provide receipts for reimbursement. Direct billing is available for some insurance providers. I can help you understand your coverage options.

Ready to Connect?

I'm here to support you and your child on this journey. Every child is unique, and I believe in creating individualized approaches that honor your child's strengths and address their challenges with compassion and expertise.
