My Approach

My therapeutic approach is built on the foundation of family-centered care, where I work collaboratively with families to understand their goals, concerns, and priorities. I believe that parents are the experts on their children, and my role is to provide the professional knowledge and tools to support their child's development.

My Approach & Expertise

My therapy approach is grounded in evidence-based practice, family-centered care, and a deep commitment to understanding each child's unique needs and strengths. I work closely with families to develop individualized treatment plans that support children in reaching their full potential while celebrating their individual differences.

Throughout my career, I have developed expertise in various areas of pediatric occupational therapy, including feeding difficulties, sensory processing challenges, fine motor development, and social-emotional regulation. I am particularly passionate about helping families navigate feeding challenges and supporting children with autism spectrum disorders through evidence-based interventions.

Core Philosophy

Every child is unique, and their therapy should be too. I believe that effective occupational therapy happens when we meet children where they are, build on their strengths, and support them in developing the skills they need to participate fully in their daily activities. My approach emphasizes collaboration, respect, and evidence-based practice to achieve meaningful outcomes for children and families.

Therapeutic Methods

I use a variety of therapeutic approaches and techniques, always selecting the methods that best meet each child's individual needs and learning style. My interventions are play-based, engaging, and designed to promote skill development in a natural and enjoyable way.

Play-Based Intervention

Using play as the primary vehicle for learning and skill development. Play is a child's natural way of exploring their world and developing new abilities.

Sensory Integration

Helping children process and respond to sensory information more effectively to improve their participation in daily activities.

Motor Learning Principles

Using evidence-based motor learning strategies to help children develop and refine their movement skills and coordination.

Behavioral Strategies

Implementing positive behavior supports and self-regulation techniques to help children manage their emotions and behaviors effectively.

Environmental Modifications

Adapting home, school, and community environments to support the child's success and independence in daily activities.

Collaborative Consultation

Working closely with teachers, daycare providers, and other professionals to ensure consistent support across all environments.
