The PLAY Project

The PLAY Project (Play and Language for Autistic Youngsters) is an evidence-based early intervention program designed to help children with autism spectrum disorders develop social communication skills through play.

What is The PLAY Project?

The PLAY Project uses a relationship-based approach that focuses on following the child's lead, building on their natural interests and motivations. Through play-based activities, children learn to engage socially, communicate effectively, and develop meaningful relationships.

How It Works

  • Individual assessment to understand your child's unique strengths and challenges
  • Customized intervention plan based on your child's needs and interests
  • Parent coaching and training to implement strategies at home
  • Regular progress monitoring and plan adjustments

My PLAY Project Certification

I am a certified PLAY Project Consultant, having completed comprehensive training in this innovative approach to supporting children with autism. This certification ensures that I provide high-quality, evidence-based intervention that follows the PLAY Project methodology.
