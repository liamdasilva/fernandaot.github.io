About Me

Hi there! I'm Fernanda, and I am a Registered Occupational Therapist with a deep passion for pediatric therapy. My journey in occupational therapy began with a desire to make a meaningful difference in the lives of children and families.

My Mission

I believe that every child deserves the opportunity to participate fully in the activities that are important to them. My mission is to help kids participate in meaningful activities while valuing integrity, family-centered care, and compassion. Whether it's learning to write their name, playing with friends, or sitting at the dinner table with their family, I'm here to support children in reaching their goals.

My Background

My approach to therapy is grounded in evidence-based practice, family-centered care, and a commitment to understanding each child's unique needs and strengths. I work closely with families to develop individualized treatment plans that support children in reaching their full potential.

Throughout my career, I have developed expertise in various areas of pediatric occupational therapy, including feeding difficulties, sensory processing challenges, fine motor development, and social-emotional regulation. I am particularly passionate about helping families navigate feeding challenges and supporting children with autism spectrum disorders through evidence-based interventions.

Specialized Training

I am committed to continuous learning and staying current with the latest evidence-based practices in pediatric occupational therapy. My specialized training includes certifications in the SOS Approach to Feeding and The PLAY Project, both of which are gold-standard interventions in their respective areas.

SOS Feeding Specialist

Certified in the Sequential Oral Sensory (SOS) Approach to Feeding, an evidence-based program for assessing and treating children with feeding difficulties and weight/growth problems.

PLAY Project Consultant

Certified PLAY Project Consultant providing relationship-based early intervention for children with autism spectrum disorders using a developmentally appropriate approach.

Sleep Specialist

Special Needs Sleep Certification from the Institute of Pediatric Sleep, helping families address sleep challenges in children with developmental differences.

Why I Love What I Do

What I love most about being an occupational therapist is witnessing the joy and confidence that comes when a child masters a new skill or overcomes a challenge. Whether it's a child taking their first bite of a new food, successfully completing a craft project, or playing independently with peers, these moments remind me why I chose this profession.

I also deeply value the relationships I build with families. Parents are the true experts on their children, and I consider myself privileged to support families on their journey. Together, we celebrate successes, navigate challenges, and work toward meaningful goals that enhance the child's quality of life.
